Plenty of strong Bangladeshi SMEs struggle to raise capital — not because the
business is weak, but because it isn't presented the way investors and lenders
actually read it. Investment readiness is mostly housekeeping done well.

## Start with the numbers

Clean, believable financials beat optimistic ones. Three things matter most:
historical performance you can defend, a forward model tied to real drivers, and
a clear view of how the money will be used and repaid or returned.

## Tell the story in their language

Investors fund a future, but they de-risk it with evidence. Pair the ambition
with proof — customers, contracts, unit economics — and be honest about what the
capital unlocks.

## Fix the structure before the pitch

Governance, ownership, and basic controls signal that the business won't waste
their money. This is usually the cheapest thing to fix and the most overlooked.
